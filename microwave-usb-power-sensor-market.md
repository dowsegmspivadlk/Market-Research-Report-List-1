Silicon Photodiodes are favored for their high sensitivity and faster response times, making them suitable for low-power applications and a wide range of wavelengths. In contrast, Germanium Photodiodes offer superior performance in the infrared spectrum, with better efficiency at higher power levels, making them ideal for industrial and high-frequency applications. Both types serve critical roles in accurately measuring microwave power in various electronic and telecommunications setups.</p></p>
